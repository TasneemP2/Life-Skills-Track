# Energy Management

## What are the activities you do that make you relax – Calm quadrant?
* I do activities that slow me down and help me breathe.
* I choose things that clear my mind and make me feel light.
* I focus on simple habits that help me recover mentally.

## When do you find yourself getting into the Stress quadrant?
* I enter the Stress quadrant when I’m learning something new or stepping out of my comfort zone.
* I feel stressed when tasks pile up or deadlines get closer.
* I notice it when situations demand more effort or attention than usual.

## How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?
* I feel a boost of energy and enthusiasm toward what I’m doing.
* I stay fully engaged and look forward to the outcome.
* I notice a positive rush that motivates me to keep going.

## Question 4: Paraphrase Sleep Is Your Superpower — only points

* Lack of sleep weakens immunity.

## Question 5: Ideas I can implement to sleep better
* Follow a fixed sleep and wake-up time.
* Keep my room dark and cool.
* Avoid screens before sleeping.
* Reduce caffeine late in the day.
* Do light exercise or stretching.
* Keep my mind calm with breathing or meditation.

## Question 6: Paraphrase Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise — minimum 5 points
* Exercise boosts mood by increasing good hormones.
* It improves focus and memory.
* Exercise reduces stress and anxiety.
* It strengthens the brain’s ability to learn.
* Regular movement protects the brain as we age.
* Exercise helps build new brain cells.

## Question 7: Steps I can take to exercise more
* Starting with small, simple workouts daily.
* Add short walks whenever possible.
* Picking exercises I enjoy so I stay consistent.
* Fixing a specific time for movement each day.
* Tracking progress to stay motivated.
* Mixing light and moderate activities like yoga, stretches, or sports.
